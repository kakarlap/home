Mini Bio

Niall Horan, also known as "Nialler" by his bandmates, is the only Irish member of the group. He was born in Mullingar, County Westmeath, Ireland to parents Bobby Horan and Maura Gallagher. His parents split when he was 5 and Niall and his brother Greg lived with their father. Niall attended St. Kenny National School and Catholic Secondary School in Mullingar. He started playing the guitar at a young age and performed in school plays and sang in school choirs as well. When he was 16, in 2010, Niall auditioned for the X Factor with Ne-Yo's "So Sick" as a soloist, but the judges put him in a group with fellow contestants, believing they would perform better. After the announcement of the band's hiatus, Niall signed a deal with Capitol Records as a solo artist. His debut single "This Town" was released in September 2016 and Niall went on to receive the award for "Favourite Breakout Artist" at the 2017 People's Choice Awards. Niall is currently focused on his independent venture as a solo artist.

Fun Fact: Before Harry came up with the name "One Direction", Niall suggested to name the band "Niall and the Potatoes".
